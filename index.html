S-03
Offer Strategy per ICP

The offer is what converts a reply into a deal. ICP + Message + Offer = pipeline. All three must be defined before a single email is sent. The first ask must never be "let us quote your next event" — it must be something they can say yes to easily.

Venue Partnerships

ElementDetail
What we offerPreferred AV Partner status — Sonus becomes the venue's recommended in-house AV supplier, referred to clients booking event space. No exclusivity required.
First askA 20-minute call to understand their current AV setup and how they handle supplier referrals. No pitch — just a conversation.
AngleVenues earn revenue or avoid complaints when their AV supplier is reliable. Sonus removes the risk of a bad client experience.
Secondary offerFree AV audit of their current event space setup — identify gaps or improvements they can offer clients.
AvoidLeading with price · Mentioning specific events · Asking for exclusivity in first message

Agencies

ElementDetail
What we offerWhite-label production support — Sonus delivers technical AV, lighting, and staging under the agency's brand. Agency keeps the client relationship and margin.
First askA brief call to understand what events they're currently producing in Scotland and whether they have a reliable technical supplier already.
AngleAgencies that win Scottish or UK-wide clients need a trusted technical partner on the ground. Sonus removes the risk of bringing in an unknown supplier.
Secondary offerOffer to quote on a live brief — to demonstrate response speed and spec quality.
AvoidCompeting with the agency for the end client · Talking equipment before understanding their brief

Brands & Corporate

ElementDetail
What we offerEnd-to-end technical event delivery — Sonus manages all AV, production, and technical logistics. One supplier, one point of contact.
First askA short call to understand their annual event calendar, how they source AV, and whether they're happy with it.
AngleA technical failure at an AGM or leadership event is visible to the most important people in the company. Sonus removes that risk.
Secondary offerFree production brief review — send us the spec for an upcoming event and we'll return a same-day response.
AvoidGeneric 'we supply great AV' messaging · Asking about budget in the first contact

AV Installations

ElementDetail
What we offerLED and AV installation audit — a free site assessment or specification review identifying what a space needs for modern AV/LED.
First askA 15-minute call to understand whether any upcoming projects have an AV or digital signage requirement at specification stage.
AngleAV requirements added late in a project and spec'd by people without technical knowledge lead to expensive changes. Getting Sonus in early saves cost.
Secondary offerOffer to review an existing or draft AV specification and provide a written improvement recommendation — no cost, no obligation.
AvoidQuoting before understanding the project · Competing on price with an incumbent
S-04
Contact Selection Logic

This is the most critical part of the system. The entire value of outreach depends on reaching the right person. Clay + OpenAI must implement the following logic before any contact enters an outreach sequence.

Contact Ranking

RankTitle CriteriaLogic
1stGroup-level decision-makerGroup Events Director, Head of Commercial Partnerships, Regional Director of Sales, VP Hospitality — select if found
2ndProperty or senior events roleEvents Manager, Director of Events, Head of Sales — use only if no group-level contact found
3rdMarketing or commercial leadMarketing Director, Commercial Director — fallback only if ranks 1 and 2 absent
SkipOperational or admin rolesBar Manager, Front of House, Reception, PA, Admin, Coordinator — NEVER contact these titles

Sequential Outreach — Multiple Contacts Same Company

Contact 1 (highest rank) → send sequence → wait reply window (14 days) No reply → pause Contact 1 → move to Contact 2 (next rank down) No reply → pause Contact 2 → move to Contact 3 Reply received at any point → STOP all other contacts at this company All contacts exhausted → mark Cold → re-engagement in 60 days

Contact Not Found — Waterfall Discovery

Step 1: Apollo — search by company + target title Step 2: Sales Navigator — filter by company + seniority + function Step 3: Domain search — pattern match work email from known domain Step 4: Google Maps — for venue/hotel properties, find contact via listing Step 5: LinkedIn enrichment — scrape public profile data Found → enrich, verify, score, add to sequence Not found → flag 'Contact Unknown' → or set to auto deep-search via Perplexity/OpenAI → log for manual review if still unresolved

Lead Scoring — A / B / C Classification

ScoreCriteriaAction
A — Strong fitCompany matches ICP, correct decision-maker title, verified email, active signalsEnter outreach sequence immediately
B — Likely fitCompany matches ICP, title adjacent (one level off), email verifiedEnter sequence — adjust messaging angle
C — Weak fitCompany partially matches ICP, title unclear, or signals ambiguousHold. Do not send. Flag for manual review.
ExcludedWrong industry, wrong tier, blacklisted, competitor, or no verified emailRemove. Do not re-add without manual override.
S-05
Business Qualification Logic

Before any contact at a company is added to a Clay workbook, Clay + OpenAI + Perplexity must research the company and run it through a qualification decision tree.

Core Qualification Decision Tree

STEP 1: What does this company do? → AI researches website, LinkedIn, and public data → Classify: Agency | Corporate | Venue | Developer | Brand | Other STEP 2: Does this company organise, manage, or run events? → YES → proceed to Step 3 → NO → EXCLUDE. Tag: 'No event activity detected' STEP 3: What type of events do they run? → Corporate events (conferences, AGMs, internal comms) → ICP: Brands & Corporate → Brand activations, experiential, exhibitions → ICP: Agencies → Venue-based events (conferences, weddings, etc) → ICP: Venue Partnerships → Public infrastructure / fit-out / LED installs → ICP: AV Installations → Cannot determine → Flag for manual review STEP 4: What outreach angle applies? → AI generates angle from company type and event activity → Stored in Clay field: outreach_angle → Passed into personalisation token for email and LinkedIn copy

Agency-Specific Qualification

Condition
Company type = Agency
Action
Run secondary check: does this agency produce, manage, or deliver live events or experiential campaigns?
Condition
Agency: event evidence found
Action
Qualify. Classify event types. Find Senior Producer / Experiential Director / Campaign Manager.
Condition
Agency: NO event evidence
Action
EXCLUDE. Tag: 'Digital/PR agency — no live event activity'. Do not re-add without manual override.

Hotel & Venue Group Logic

STEP 1: Is this property part of a parent group? → AI researches ownership: Cameron House → Frasers Hospitality | Gleneagles → Ennismore STEP 2: Is the parent group already in the system? → YES → link property to existing group record. No duplicates. → NO → create group-level record. Map all known properties. STEP 3: Find group-level contact, not property-level → Target: Group Events Director, Head of Commercial Partnerships at group HQ → Do NOT contact: individual hotel GM, local events coordinator

S-08
Data & Lead Layer

Clay Workbooks — 4 ICPs

  • One workbook per ICP: Venue Partnerships, Agencies, AV Installations, Brands & Corporate
  • Full waterfall discovery and copy based on research — no contact enters a sequence without being fully researched
  • All workbooks share a master contact log — deduplication runs across ALL workbooks before any contact is added
⛔
The same record must NEVER appear in multiple campaigns sending multiple angles to the same person.

15,000+ Contact Database Clean-Up

  • Import all existing CSV data into a single unified Clay table
  • Deduplicate — significant overlap expected across existing sheets
  • Run all contacts through the qualification decision tree (Section 5)
  • Update job titles — validate against LinkedIn and company websites
  • Re-verify all emails: IcyPeas → Million Verifier two-step
  • Remove personal emails — business addresses only. No gmail, hotmail, yahoo.
  • Score and tag into 4 ICP categories with location
  • Remove contacts at closed companies, irrelevant organisations, or excluded tiers

Email Verification — Two-Step Process

Step 1: IcyPeas (primary) — €89 / 10,000 credits Verified → proceed | Uncertain → pass to Step 2 Step 2: Million Verifier (secondary) — credits owned by Sonus Events Verified → proceed | Risky / invalid → remove. Do not attempt outreach.

Automation & Integration — N8N

N8N is already running on a self-hosted Contabo VPS at server.sonusevents.com. All automation must be built within this existing instance. The contractor must not provision a separate N8N environment.

Core Automation Flows

  • Clay → email platform: verified + scored A/B leads pushed to correct campaign
  • Email platform → Clay: reply tags, bounce flags, engagement scores written back
  • Email platform → GHL: positive replies and interested contacts pushed to GHL pipeline
  • LinkedIn reply → GHL: HeyReach positive replies also routed to GHL
  • Form completion → GHL: qualification form triggers Discovery Call stage + Gillian notification
  • RingOver → GHL: call outcomes logged against contact record
  • Re-engagement: 60-day retry logic, job change check, LinkedIn connection trigger

Suppression Automation

  • Blacklist check: runs before ANY contact is added to ANY sequence — no exceptions
  • Competitor check: runs before ANY company enters ANY workbook
  • Group suppression: N8N checks parent_group_id and group_suppression fields before sending
  • Active conversation check: if active_conversation = TRUE at correct entity level, block send

Jarvis AI Assistant

Jarvis is the AI assistant that sits on top of Jacko and all connected systems. It is the primary interaction layer between Alan and the full technology stack. Jarvis does not replace individual tools — it orchestrates them.

Core Capabilities

Orchestrate workflowsTrigger and monitor N8N workflows, Clay tasks, Claude Code jobs, and GHL automations from a single interface
Tool bridgingConnect local tools (Adobe, Canva, Resolume) with cloud services via automations and agents

Approval escalationFlag actions that require human approval before executing — never acts unilaterally on SEND or SPEND
Draft & iterateGenerate first drafts of emails, proposals, social posts, briefs — human reviews before any go live

Permissions Model

Approval Escalation Flow

Jarvis prepares action → classifies type (READ / WRITE / SEND / SPEND) READ → execute automatically WRITE → execute → log → notify (no approval needed) SEND → HOLD → send approval request to Alan/Lauren via notification → Approved → execute → log → Rejected → cancel → log reason → No response in 24hrs → cancel → escalate SPEND → HOLD → send approval request to Alan ONLY

Routing Decision Tree

Is the data sensitive / confidential? YES → route to local model on Jacko (Llama/Mistral) NO → proceed Is this a bulk / low-quality-threshold task? YES → GPT-4o-mini or local model NO → proceed Does this require real-time web data? YES → Perplexity API NO → proceed Does this require code generation or autonomous execution? YES → Claude Code NO → proceed Does this require deep reasoning, long context, or proposal quality? YES → Claude Sonnet NO → GPT-4o

Self-Healing Logic

If a Clay workflow fails 3 times in a row: → Pause the workflow → send alert → log to PostgreSQL on Jacko If a model returns poor-quality outputs 5 times on the same task type: → Flag for prompt review → route to next-tier model as fallback If email bounce rate exceeds threshold (Section 7.3): → Auto-pause the sending domain → re-verify list segment → alert team

SEO, AEO, PR & Backlinks

SEO Workflows

  • Keyword research: AI-assisted identification per service and location (Glasgow AV hire, Edinburgh event production etc.)

Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO)

AEO targets AI-powered search tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini). The goal is for Sonus Events to appear as a credible answer when someone asks an AI about AV production in Scotland.

  • Create structured, factual content pages answering specific questions: 'best AV company Glasgow', 'LED screen hire Edinburgh'
  • Schema markup on all service pages and FAQ sections
  • Build citations and mentions on trusted industry sources that AI models are trained on
  • Monitor AI search tool responses quarterly — test whether Sonus appears in relevant answers

PR & Backlink Generation

  • AI identifies backlink opportunities: industry publications, event directories, Scottish business press
  • Claude drafts personalised PR outreach emails per target publication
  • Human reviews and approves all PR outreach before sending
  • PR outreach tracked in GHL — separate pipeline from sales outreach
S-22
Websites, Landing Pages & UX/UI

Design Principles

  • Ultra-modern but functional: dark aesthetic, strong typography, minimal but purposeful animation
  • Conversion-first: every page has a clear primary CTA and secondary action
  • Mobile-first: 60%+ of traffic is likely mobile — design mobile first, enhance for desktop
  • Performance: Core Web Vitals targets — LCP under 2.5s, CLS under 0.1, FID under 100ms
  • Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA minimum compliance

Landing Pages for Paid Ads

  • Each paid ad campaign requires a dedicated landing page — not the homepage
  • Landing page must match the ad creative exactly: same headline, same offer, same visual language
  • Minimum tracking: Google Analytics 4 conversion event, Meta Pixel, Google Ads conversion tag
  • A/B testing hook on every landing page: at minimum two headline variants
  • Page speed target: under 2 seconds on mobile

AI-Assisted Build

  • Claude Code generates HTML/CSS/JS components from design briefs — human developer reviews and integrates
  • AI analyses competitor websites and identifies UX improvements for Sonus
  • AI generates SEO-optimised copy for all pages — human reviews and approves before publishing
  • Future: Nginx on Jacko for hosting — phase in once server stability proven

S-24
Proposals, Tenders & Quotes

Proposal Generation Workflow

Input: client brief (text) + ICP data from Clay + historical pricing → Claude analyses brief: event type, scale, technical requirements → Claude matches requirements against Sonus capability library → Claude drafts: executive summary, scope, equipment, team, pricing, timeline → Claude Code generates formatted document (Word/PDF) using Sonus template → Human reviews: checks accuracy, adjusts pricing, adds bespoke details → Human approves → sends to client

Versioning & Approval Workflow

  • All proposals stored on Jacko with version control (v1, v2, v3 etc.)
  • GHL tracks proposal status: Draft → Internal Review → Sent → Accepted / Declined
  • No proposal sent without Alan or Lauren sign-off — Jarvis must request approval before sending
  • Accepted proposals trigger onboarding sequence in GHL automatically
  • Declined proposals: reason captured and flagged for ICP learning loop

Output Formats

  • Word (.docx): editable version for internal use and client negotiation
  • PDF: final locked version for formal submission
  • Slide deck (.pptx): for presentations and pitches
  • All outputs generated via Claude Code using approved Sonus templates stored on Jacko

3D Visualisation Pipeline

Pipeline

INPUT: iPhone LiDAR scan of venue / space (Polycam app) → Stage 1: Export from Polycam (.OBJ / .FBX / .USDZ) → Stage 2: Mesh cleanup in Blender — remove artefacts, simplify geometry → Stage 3: Import into Unreal Engine 5 — scene setup, materials, base lighting → Stage 4: Add AV/lighting/staging elements — LED screens, speakers, trusses, draping → Stage 5: Lighting design — match real-world fixture spec from event plan → Stage 6: Render outputs — stills, walkthrough video, interactive file (optional) OUTPUT: Stills (4K PNG) · Walkthrough Video (MP4) · Interactive UE5 build (optional)
